A Book of Frictions

50 global creative practices examined through the lens of deliberate friction — the resistance that produces depth in an age of instant generation.

Scroll to begin ↓

In a world where creativity is under pressure, I wanted to look outward — across disciplines, borders, and generations — to understand how different practitioners think, work, and make.

I wanted to find the themes that cross boundaries. To ask how they hold up against AI. To consider what it might look like if they united around human craft, human scale, and process.

What follows is a study of 51 practices across 9 disciplines, 15 countries, and 6 decades. It became an argument for friction.

The thesis

The friction is the point

AI's entire value proposition is the elimination of friction — seamless, instant, frictionless generation. Every practice in this study introduces friction deliberately. The resistance is the work.

The journey is the value. The output is the receipt. What looks replicable by AI is not, because it is not about the output — it is about the process, the rituals, the relationships, the accidents, and the accumulated human decisions that produced it. Without skin in the game there is no passion. Without passion nothing convinces. Without the work you don't get to something worth fighting for.

Three voices

"Baking bread is not about controlling the process; it's about guiding the fermentation. You are there to act as a catalyst for what the dough naturally wants to do." Chad Robertson, Tartine — Food, San Francisco
"One artisan cannot finish the work of another artisan." Matthieu Blazy, Chanel — Fashion, Paris
"Emoticons don't have wrinkles. Authored craft does." Luna Maurer & Roel Wouters, Moniker — Interactive Design, Amsterdam

A baker in San Francisco. A fashion designer in Paris. An interactive studio in Amsterdam. Three disciplines, three materials, one shared principle: the human journey through resistant material produces something generation cannot.
