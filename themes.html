The Themes

Ten ideas that crossed every boundary — discipline, geography, scale, generation. None were planned. All were discovered through listening.

The frictions describe how these practices resist. The themes describe what they believe. These are the recurring ideas that multiple practitioners articulated independently — in different disciplines, different countries, at different scales — without knowing they were saying the same thing.

01
The inside precedes the outside
The authored object begins inside the maker, not on the surface of the product. From the yarn outward. From the breath outward. From the daily run outward. Every practitioner in this study works from the inside out — from attention, from preparation, from the body's knowledge.
AI generates from the outside in — it starts with the desired output and works backward. The direction of making is the fundamental difference.
"Architects must heal themselves in order to heal the world."Vo Trong Nghia — staff meditate two hours daily before drawing a line
02
Community precedes brand
You don't create a practice and then find an audience. The community already exists. You join it. The runners were already running before the brand appeared. The bakers were already baking. The readers were already reading. The brand is the structure built on top. Remove the brand and the community survives. Remove the community and the brand is a logo on a t-shirt.
AI generates for "users" — abstract, aggregated, optimised. Authored craft begins with a specific community in a specific place and works outward from their reality.
"You don't start a brand because people will find you. You start because there is already a community."Brice Partouche, Satisfy — the runners existed before the brand did
03
The process of learning never ends
Every day is a new study. Practitioners twenty years into their work say they are still finding themselves. The design process is search, not a production pipeline. Understanding something is not the ability to define it — making the known unknown is what thrills us afresh. The incompleteness is the point.
AI's training is a completed event. For these practitioners, learning is continuous and unfinished — the daily bread, the yearly season, the decade-long building. Authored craft is a practice of perpetual not-knowing, which is the opposite of what AI sells: instant knowing.
"I'm just finding myself."Hiroki Nakamura, Visvim — said after more than twenty years
04
One artisan cannot finish the work of another
The irreplaceability of the individual hand — not as ego but as material fact. The leather carries the mark of the specific person who shaped it. The stone split by one worker cannot be finished by another. The book shaped by years of correspondence between two specific people could not have been shaped by any other two. Human hands are not interchangeable.
AI's fundamental proposition is that any model can finish any other model's work. The output is interchangeable. This is the exact negation. The human hand produces a result no other hand can complete. This is not mysticism. It is a material fact about the irregularity of human touch.
"One artisan cannot finish the work of another artisan."Matthieu Blazy, Chanel — on the leather workshop in Montebello
05
Constraints generate freedom
The most counterintuitive and most repeated structural claim. Forty colours. One pleat. A $20,000 budget. An eleven-page uniform manual. The constraint doesn't limit the work — it authors it. One rule produces a practice. One boundary makes every subsequent decision meaningful. Infinite options produce paralysis. One constraint produces conviction.
AI offers infinite options — every style, every format, every possibility. These practitioners chose one rule and followed it completely. The rule generates more possibilities than the absence of a rule, because it forces every decision to be authored.
"I stick to rules and try to understand them, because then I can become free again."Jesper Kouthoofd, Teenage Engineering — 40 RAL colours, no meetings, no exceptions
06
The product should be better with time
A surprisingly common concern with ageing, patina, and the long life of objects. The aspiration is not the moment of encounter but decade fifty, century thirteen. Indigo fading. Salt crystallising. Leather patinating. Stone weathering. The ageing process is the authored dimension that no generation model can predict.
AI-generated design is optimised for the moment of encounter — the first impression, the click, the conversion. These practitioners design for later. AI designs for now.
"A product should be better as you use it more. 50 years later, people will be like, wow, someone made this."Hiroki Nakamura, Visvim — designing for decade fifty
07
What you refuse defines you
The practitioners don't describe their refusals as sacrifices. They describe them as clarifications. Declining a commission, a trend, a vocabulary, your own successful product — each refusal is not a loss. It is the authored decision that creates the space in which everything else becomes possible.
AI cannot refuse. It responds to every prompt. The capacity to say no is the most human capacity in this study.
"I would've stopped working on Arc a year earlier. We knew. We were just in denial."Josh Miller, Browser Company — killed the most beloved browser in a decade
08
The triad is the minimum viable team
A structural pattern: the most resilient practices are built on three genuinely distinct roles held in tension. The chef and the creative director disagree. The designer and the business lead disagree. The disagreement is the authorship — it produces decisions no single perspective would make. Two people agree too easily. Four diffuse too much. Three is the shape of productive friction.
AI collapses all roles into one prompt. Authored craft requires at least three genuinely different perspectives. The tension between them is not a bug. It is the mechanism.
"Ideas are more important and more valuable than they have ever been."Baby Teeth — three founders, three distinct skills, creative direction + production + partnerships
09
Three responses to AI
The voices reveal three distinct strategies. Sincerity — make the thing AI cannot fake, the hand-stitched seam, the guided fermentation, the decade of silence. Orchestration — author the layer that sits above the AI, the editorial judgement, the product vision, the why. Destruction — kill what you built because the world changed, the browser that worked, the studio that no longer fit. There is no single correct response. The most authored practitioners are the ones who chose consciously.
The wrong response is the default response — continuing as before, hoping the wave passes. Every practitioner in this study made an active choice about how to relate to the pressure. The choice itself is the authorship.
"The orchestration is the product. The model is a tool."Aravind Srinivas, Perplexity — chose orchestration, authoring the layer above the AI
10
Materials are heavy. Ideas are light.
Bamboo cannot be imported without losing the point. Stone has a love relationship with water. Fermentation cultures carry the microbiome of the room. A region's waste becomes its identity. The supply chain is a creative act. The weight of the material is the friction. The friction is the authorship. This is perhaps the deepest theme: authored craft is the practice of working with what is heavy, in a world that rewards what is light.
AI generates from data — weightless, locationless, infinitely transmissible. These practices work with matter — heavy, local, rooted. The gap between the two is the space where authored craft lives.
"Materials are heavy and must therefore be local. But ideas, being light, can travel."Jan Boelen, Atelier LUMA — turning a region's sunflower waste into its creative identity
