Seven Frictions

Every practice introduces friction deliberately. The research identified seven modes — seven ways of resisting the frictionless. Scroll deeper.

Friction operates on three nested layers. The practitioner carries it in their biography, their spiritual practice, their accumulated time. The process carries it in the materials they use, the research they do, the economic constraints they accept. The output carries it in how the work communicates, ages, and reveals itself.

The seven friction modes describe how friction is introduced across these layers. Some modes operate primarily on the practitioner (The Withheld, The Embodied). Some on the process (The Excavated, The Constrained). Some on the relationship between the work and the world (The Refused, The Orchestrated, The Tested). Together, they form a taxonomy of deliberate resistance.

Below, the seven modes are presented as strata — geological layers that darken as you scroll deeper, from the light surface of The Withheld to the dark bedrock of The Tested. Each layer carries the practitioners who work within it and the friction they introduce.
