When they were founded

The 2000–2019 generation

75% of entries were founded between 2000 and 2019 — the two decades between the iPhone and ChatGPT. This generation grew up alongside digital culture but before AI became generative. That window is now closed.

Only 5 entries were founded in the 2020s. The pre-1990 entries — Pentagram, Wolff Olins, Miyake, Zumthor — are the elders.

What they do

Discipline breakdown

Fashion and Brand & Identity are the largest categories. But architecture, though only 6 entries, carries the thesis disproportionately — all three of the most AI-resistant practices are architects.

AI vulnerability

Average vulnerability by discipline

Food and Architecture are the most AI-resistant. Technology and Brand & Identity are the most vulnerable. The further from the physical, the closer to AI replicability.

Score based on four dimensions: friction mode, practitioner layer, process layer, and output layer. Lower = more resistant.

The full spectrum

Every practice, scored

Each row is a practice. The bar length is the AI vulnerability score. Teal = resistant. Coral = vulnerable. The three architects at the top score 5. The two systems practices at the bottom score 65.

How they sustain

Economic model

Client services and independent are the two dominant models — together 51% of all entries. VC-backed practices are a minority (10%), and the 7 entries inside larger brands can only use three of seven friction modes.

The friction modes

Distribution across the seven modes

The Constrained is the most common primary mode (9 practices). The modes are remarkably evenly distributed — no single mode dominates, confirming that friction takes many forms.
